# About me

Hello! I am Zehan yang, a Ph.D. in Statistics graduated from
[University of Connecticut](https://uconn.edu).

I currently serve as a statistical reviewer at the [U.S. Food and Drug 
Administration](https://www.fda.gov), [Center for Biologics Evaluation and 
Research](https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/fda-organization/center-biologics-evaluation-and-research-cber), 
where I review submissions related to Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls (CMC) statistics,
as well as clinical statistics for medical devices.

My research interests are Survival Analysis, Optimal
Subsampling and Statistical Computing. I have been working on the
optimal subsampling method for big survival data based on parametric
and semi-parametric accelerated failure time model for three years.

Besides my own research, I was a graduate assistant at
[The Cato T. Laurencin Institute for Regenerative 
Engineering](https://health.uconn.edu/regenerative-engineering-institute/)
in the [University of Connecticut Health
Center](https://health.uconn.edu) from Septemper, 2021 to December, 2023. 
I have finished multiple statistical consulting projects for medical 
researchers and medical doctors.
